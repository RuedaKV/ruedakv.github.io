Journalism Comments Scrapers

Purpose

Northwestern's Computational Journalism Lab was studying the reactions and behaviors of newsreaders exposed to predictive journalism methods and models.

Predictive journalism models range from predicting COVID-19 death tolls in the United States, to predicting the odds of teams to win the World Series, to predicting the results of the 2020 elections.

Despite the robustness behind these models, their useage in news and journalism is relatively new leading to questions about how newsreaders react and respond to them.

Goal

Aggregate as many user comments as possible from dozens of news articles that include predictive journalism models.

Outcome

I developed a series of Python scripts that can scrape user comments from any article from The New York Times, FiveThirtyEight, and The WashingtonPost. The scripts were used to scrape over 22,000 user comments from a collection of articles. The scripts also include the ability to format and write scraped comments into a Google Sheets.

Tools and Technologies

Scripts developed in Python using Pandas, Selenium, and the Google Sheets API.
